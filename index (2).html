Who I Am

I am an experimental researcher working at the frontier of quantum photonics and 2D materials physics. Currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at CEMES & LPCNO (Toulouse), my work spans the full pipeline from nanofabrication and heterostructure assembly to cryogenic optical spectroscopy and quantum light characterisation.

My doctoral research at CNRS-LNCMI focused on magneto-optical spectroscopy of TMDC heterostructures under extreme magnetic fields up to 70 T, revealing twist-angle-dependent exciton physics and Moire effects.

I am passionate about bridging fundamental quantum optics and device-level photonic integration, building tomorrow's quantum technologies one photon at a time.

Key Research Projects

NV-Center Quantum Sensing & Dielectric Nanoantenna Integration
AFM Nanoxerography-based deterministic placement of nanodiamonds with NV centers into dielectric nanoantennas and metasurfaces. g2(tau) autocorrelation measurements confirm single-photon purity. Fourier-plane imaging for far-field emission pattern mapping.
NV Centersg2(t) HBTAFM NanoxerographySingle-Photon EmissionMetasurfaces
k-Space Vector Filtering for Spin-Dark/Bright Exciton Separation
Fourier-plane microscopy with engineered spatial frequency masks to selectively transmit in-plane or out-of-plane dipole emission lobes. K/K valley angular momentum selection rules in MoS2 used to map emission anisotropy in reciprocal space. Room-temperature spin-dark excitonic emission via high-NA objective (NA > 1.4).

TMDC Heterostructure Fabrication & AFM Ironing
Novel fabrication route for MoSe2/MoS2 heterostructures with AFM ironing post-processing to flatten bubbles and enhance PL quantum yield. Revealed twist-angle-dependent dehybridization of momentum-indirect interlayer excitons.

Ultrafast Charge Transfer in Perovskite-TMDC Heterostructures
Polarised pump-probe and transient absorption spectroscopy on WSe2-BAPI hybrid heterostructures. Sub-picosecond charge transfer via Dexter-like coupling and emergence of new excitonic species at the organic-2D interface.

Fibre-based flake probe at 2 K and 70 T pulsed magnetic field. RTD calibration, micro-soldering of pickup coils, and polarisation-resolved PL on TMDC flakes under extreme conditions.
